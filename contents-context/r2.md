### 1) What’s the difference between an AI chatbot and an AI agent?

An **AI chatbot** is primarily designed to **answer questions** and guide users through a conversation. It’s great for FAQs, basic support, product discovery, and document-based Q&A. An **AI agent** goes further: it can **take actions** inside your tools (create tickets, update CRM fields, generate quotes, schedule appointments, trigger workflows, or produce reports).

In real business operations, the highest ROI usually comes from agents because they reduce manual work end-to-end. For example, instead of only answering “What documents do I need to file a claim?”, an agent can also **collect the data**, **validate it**, and **open a claim ticket** with structured fields.

PingPal builds both — and recommends the right approach based on your workflow. If your goal is speed and deflection, start with a chatbot. If your goal is automation and measurable time savings, build an agent.

### 4) Can an AI chatbot use my company documents and answer accurately?

Yes — when it’s built correctly. The best practice is **knowledge grounding**, often called **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)**. Instead of “guessing,” the chatbot retrieves relevant information from your documents (SOPs, policies, pricing sheets, manuals, internal FAQs) and answers using that content as reference.

This approach is ideal when your business knowledge changes frequently or lives in multiple documents. It also makes updates easier: you refresh the knowledge base instead of rebuilding the bot.

PingPal typically sets up a clean knowledge pipeline: document ingestion, chunking, search relevance, and quality evaluation — plus guardrails for sensitive topics. The result is an AI assistant that’s useful, accurate, and aligned to your operations instead of being a generic “chat widget.”

### 5) How do you prevent hallucinations in AI systems?

You don’t “promise zero hallucinations” — you engineer the system to **reduce risk and control outcomes**. In production AI, reliability comes from:

* **Grounding** answers in your documents (RAG)
* **Guardrails** for restricted topics and risky outputs
* **Tool-based actions** (pull data from systems instead of guessing)
* **Confidence checks** and “I don’t know” fallbacks
* **Human escalation** for complex or sensitive requests
* **Testing and monitoring** using real-world scenarios

PingPal treats accuracy like a product requirement. We build evaluation sets, test edge cases, track failure patterns, and improve continuously. If your use case is regulated or sensitive (insurance, finance, healthcare admin workflows), we also add role-based access, audit logs, and strict data boundaries.

### 8) What is RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) and why does it matter?

RAG is a method that makes AI answers **more accurate and more useful** for business. Instead of relying only on a model’s general training, RAG retrieves relevant information from your own knowledge sources (documents, FAQs, product sheets, SOPs) and uses that content to generate the response.

Why it matters: businesses don’t need generic AI. They need AI that knows **their rules, their process, their wording, and their truth**. RAG also improves maintainability because when knowledge changes, you update the content source — not the entire system.

PingPal builds RAG systems with a focus on production reliability: clean ingestion, relevance tuning, safe citation behavior (when needed), and monitoring. If your chatbot or agent must represent your brand and avoid misinformation, RAG is usually the foundation.

### 9) Should I fine-tune a model or use RAG?

Most business use cases should start with **RAG**, not fine-tuning. RAG is faster to build, easier to update, and typically delivers strong accuracy when you have documents and structured knowledge. Fine-tuning makes sense when you need consistent style, specialized behavior, or domain-specific patterns — and you have enough high-quality training data to justify it.

A simple way to decide:

* If your main need is “answer using our documents accurately,” choose **RAG**.
* If your need is “behave like our best agent in every scenario,” consider **fine-tuning** — after you validate ROI.

PingPal often uses a hybrid approach: start with RAG for speed and accuracy, then selectively fine-tune or add specialized classifiers once the system is proven and usage data is available.
